Melon Soup

Description

Egusi soup is a flavorful West African dish, popular in Nigeria and Ghana, made from ground melon seeds that create a thick, nutty base. Cooked with vegetables like spinach, proteins such as goat or fish, and spiced with palm oil and crayfish, it is typically served with pounded yam or rice. This hearty soup is a regional favorite, perfect for any occasion.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of melon seeds
  • 1 cup of palm oil
  • 1 cup of crayfish
  • 1/2 bunch of spinach
  • 1kg of goat meat
  • 1/2kg of chicken
  • 1 smoked catfish
  • 1 bulb of onions
  • 1/4 cup of pepper
  • 4 cubes of stock

Steps

  1. Wash and season the goat meat and chicken with onions, pepper, and stock cubes.
  2. Boil the meat until tender, then set aside.
  3. Blend the melon seeds and crayfish together to form a smooth paste.
  4. Heat the palm oil in a pot, add the blended melon seeds and crayfish, and fry for 10 minutes.
  5. Add the meat, stock, and smoked fish to the pot, and cook for another 10 minutes.
  6. Wash and chop the spinach, then add it to the pot and cook for an additional 5 minutes.
  7. Stir the soup, and adjust the seasoning to taste.
  8. Simmer for 5 minutes, then serve hot with pounded yam or rice.
