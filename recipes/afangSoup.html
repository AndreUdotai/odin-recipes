Afang Soup

Description

Afang soup is a nutritious Nigerian delicacy from the South-South region, made with ground Afang leaves and waterleaf for a thick, earthy texture. Packed with proteins like beef or fish, and enriched with periwinkles and palm oil, itâ€™s often enjoyed with fufu or pounded yam. This vibrant soup is a wholesome favorite for any meal.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of ground Afang leaves
  • 1 bunch of waterleaf
  • 1/2 cup of palm oil
  • 1 cup of periwinkles
  • 1kg of beef
  • 1 smoked fish
  • 1 bulb of onions
  • 1/4 cup of pepper
  • 3 stock cubes
  • 1/2 cup of crayfish

Steps

  1. Season and boil the beef with onions, pepper, and stock cubes until tender.
  2. Wash and chop the waterleaf, then add it to the pot with the meat stock.
  3. Stir in the palm oil and crayfish, cooking for 5 minutes.
  4. Add the ground Afang leaves, smoked fish, and periwinkles, then simmer for 10 minutes.
  5. Adjust seasoning to taste and cook for another 5 minutes.
  6. Serve hot with pounded yam or fufu.

Enjoy your meal!
